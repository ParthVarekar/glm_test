Chapter I — Origins

A boy from the island, with the world in his feet.

Born on the 5th of February, 1985, in the parish of Santo António, Cristiano Ronaldo dos Santos Aveiro was named after Ronald Reagan — his father's favorite actor. By twelve, he had left his family in Madeira for Lisbon. By sixteen, Sporting's coaches had to pull him out of training because he was outplaying the first team. By seventeen, he was debuting for them. The continent was already watching.

Chapter II — The Theatre of Dreams

Six years that forged a myth.

Sir Alex Ferguson paid €19 million for a skinny winger after a single friendly. "He's the most exciting young player I've ever seen," Ferguson said. In Manchester, the wiry teenager became a force of nature — three Premier League titles, a Champions League crown won in the Moscow rain, and the first Ballon d'Or of his career in 2008. The boy was now the best player in the world.

Chapter III — The Galáctico

Nine years. Four Champions Leagues. An empire.

For a world-record €94 million, he came to the Bernabéu and rewrote every record book in white. Four Champions League titles in five seasons — including the impossible three-peat from 2016 to 2018. He became Real Madrid's all-time leading scorer with 450 goals in 438 matches, a ratio that defies arithmetic. Madrid had found their king. Football had found its measuring stick.

Chapter IV — New Kingdoms

Italy. A homecoming. And still, the hunger.

In Turin he conquered Italy — two Serie A titles, two Italian Super Cups, the Capocannoniere. Then came the return every Red Devil had prayed for: Old Trafford, 2021. The homecoming ended sooner than anyone wished, but the goals never stopped. By the time he left Europe, he had scored more goals than any man in the recorded history of the sport.
